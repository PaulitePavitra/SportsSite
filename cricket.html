Rules & Regulations of Cricket

  • Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each.
  • The match is played on an oval or circular field with a rectangular pitch at the center.
  • The pitch is 22 yards (20.12 m) long and 10 feet (3.05 m) wide.
  • Each team takes turns to bat and bowl.
  • A match is divided into overs, each consisting of 6 legal deliveries.
  • Runs are scored by:
    • Running between the wickets
    • Hitting boundaries (4 runs)
    • Hitting the ball over the boundary (6 runs)
  • A batsman can be dismissed by:
    • Bowled
    • Caught
    • Leg Before Wicket (LBW)
    • Run Out
    • Stumped
    • Hit Wicket
  • Common match formats:
    • Test Cricket – up to 5 days
    • One Day Internationals (ODI) – 50 overs per side
    • T20 Cricket – 20 overs per side
  • The team scoring the most runs wins the match.

Cricket Field – Positions & Dimensions

  • The cricket field has no fixed size, but usually ranges between 65–90 meters in diameter.
  • The boundary is marked by a rope or line.
  • Important fielding positions include:
    • Slip, Gully, Point, Cover
    • Mid-off, Mid-on, Square Leg
    • Fine Leg, Third Man, Long On, Long Off
    • Wicket-Keeper (behind the batsman)
  • The pitch markings include:
    • Batting creases
    • Bowling creases
    • Popping creases
